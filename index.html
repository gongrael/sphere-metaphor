Simple oscillator: Electrostatic Spheres

Simple Harmonic Oscillator

In this example of a simple harmonic oscillator, we have two spheres which exert both a repulsive and attractive force on each other. In this proposed system, repulsive forces (blue arrows) are stronger than attractive forces (red arrows) when the spheres are close to each other. The net force (purple arrows), shows the summation of the two forces, and how this changes with the distance between the spheres. In this system, there exists an equilibrium distance where the net force is equal to zero. When the system is disturbed, it will oscillate around this equilibrium position, with changes to the systems energy obsevered. What happens to the motion over time? Why?

Try pushing the spheres together or pulling the spheres apart. Observe the relationship between the two spheres and changes to potential energy. What else do you notice? Try moving the spheres as far away from each other as possible. What happens?
